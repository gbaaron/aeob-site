Our Story

How three passionate PBA fans turned late-night basketball debates into the Philippines' longest-running basketball history podcast — and found a fourth voice along the way.

The Founding of AEOB

[PLACEHOLDER] An Eternity of Basketball was born out of a shared obsession. Charlie Cuna, Sid Ventura, and Noel Zarate — three lifelong PBA fans — had spent years arguing about the greatest conferences, debating forgotten players, and reliving classic moments that most fans had never seen. In [PLACEHOLDER YEAR], they decided to stop keeping those conversations to themselves and start recording them. What began as a casual experiment quickly became something bigger: a podcast dedicated to preserving and celebrating the rich, often overlooked history of the Philippine Basketball Association.

[PLACEHOLDER] The early days were scrappy. Armed with basic equipment and an encyclopedic knowledge of PBA lore, the trio recorded episodes wherever they could — living rooms, coffee shops, late-night video calls. But the passion was undeniable, and listeners noticed. Word spread through basketball communities, social media groups, and locker-room conversations. Before long, AEOB had carved out a unique space in Philippine sports media.

[PLACEHOLDER] Then came Jay Mercado. A fellow PBA historian with his own deep well of knowledge and a knack for storytelling, Jay joined the crew as the fourth host, bringing fresh perspectives and new energy. With Jay on board, AEOB evolved — the debates got sharper, the research went deeper, and the show found another gear entirely. Together, the four hosts have built something that goes far beyond a podcast: a living archive of Philippine basketball history.

Meet the Hosts

The voices behind An Eternity of Basketball

CC

Co-Founder & Host

A lifelong PBA fan with an almost photographic memory for stats, lineups, and game details stretching back decades, Charlie brings an unmatched depth of knowledge to every episode. When he's not recording, he's usually deep in a rabbit hole researching some forgotten conference from the 1980s.

Co-Founder & Host

Host

What We Cover

From the league's founding to today's modern era, we leave no era unexplored

The Pioneer Era

The founding years of the PBA, the original franchises, and the players who laid the foundation for professional basketball in the Philippines.

The Golden Age

The explosive growth of the league, the rise of legendary dynasties, fierce rivalries, and the era that cemented the PBA as the country's most beloved sports league.

The Import Era

How foreign reinforcements changed the game, unforgettable import-local tandems, and the wild stories from the era of towering guest players.

Rivalries & Controversies

The heated matchups, buzzer-beaters, bench-clearing incidents, and off-court dramas that defined generations of PBA basketball.

Legendary Players & Coaches

Deep-dive profiles on the greatest figures in PBA history — from the all-time greats to the unsung heroes who deserve their place in the spotlight.

The Modern PBA

How the league has evolved, the new generation of stars, and what today's PBA owes to the decades of history that came before.
